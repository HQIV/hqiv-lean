\begin{abstract}
We state the strongest \emph{ratio-level} first-principles statement for baryon asymmetry currently supported by the HQIV-LEAN library, positioned after the trilogy on rapidity/\(\mathrm{Spin}(8)\) closure \cite{hqiv-so8-paper}, three-dimensional null-shell growth and octonionic gauge packaging \cite{hqiv-3d-growth-paper}, and the discrete octonionic action layer \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper}.
The formal spine is entirely \textbf{ratio-level}: discrete shell combinatorics define a cumulative curvature integral \(I(n)\); horizon ratios \(\Omega_k(n;N)=I(n)/I(N)\) are machine-checked with proved monotonicity in \(n\) at fixed \(N\); a distinguished lock-in shell \(m_{\mathrm{lockin}}\) (the library pin \texttt{referenceM}) calibrates \(\Omega_k=1\) at the lock-in horizon; and the baryon-asymmetry readout \(\eta(n;N)\) is \textbf{defined} to share that same normalization, pointwise in \((n,N)\), with an optional single anchor for the absolute scale.
Big-bang nucleosynthesis is \textbf{out of scope} as a Lean theorem: standard BBN takes the same \(\eta\) convention fixed at lock-in as input and computes light-element abundances via external Einstein--Boltzmann and nucleosynthesis networks; verifying those abundances against observation is numerical physics in companion pipelines, not part of the proof graph traced here.
What we \emph{do} claim, with file-level precision, is that baryogenesis bookkeeping is not an independent knob: it is the same partial curvature imprint that appears in the growth channel, triality-sensitive CP-bias scaffolds, and the HQVM Friedmann compatibility package---with explicit honesty boundaries where SM transport, washout, and sphaleron dynamics remain narrative targets rather than formalized theorems.
\end{abstract}

\section{Horizon ratios \(\Omega_k\) and lock-in calibration}

Define the cumulative partial integral
\[
I(n) \;:=\; \sum_{m=0}^{n-1} |\sigma(m)|
\]
(\lean{curvature_integral}).
For horizons \(N\) with \(I(N)>0\), the library defines
\[
\Omega_k(n;N) \;:=\; \frac{I(n)}{I(N)}.
\]
At the lock-in shell, Lean proves positivity \(I(m_{\mathrm{lockin}})>0\) and the calibration
\[
\Omega_k(m_{\mathrm{lockin}};m_{\mathrm{lockin}})=1
\]
(\lean{omega_k_lockin_calibration}).
This is the precise sense in which ``spatial curvature lock-in'' is a \textbf{theorem}: it is normalization of the discrete imprint integral at the declared reference horizon, not an experimental input.

\begin{proposition}[Monotonicity and approach to lock-in, Lean]
\label{prop:mono-lockin}
Assume \(I(N)>0\). Then for all \(n_1\le n_2\), \(\Omega_k(n_1;N)\le \Omega_k(n_2;N)\), with strict inequality when \(n_1<n_2\) \textup{(\lean{omega_k_at_horizon_mono}, \lean{omega_k_at_horizon_strict_mono})}.
If additionally \(n\le N\), then \(\Omega_k(n;N)\le 1\) \textup{(\lean{omega_k_at_horizon_le_one_of_le})}.
In particular, along shells \(n\le m_{\mathrm{lockin}}\) with horizon \(N=m_{\mathrm{lockin}}\), the ratio \(\Omega_k(n;m_{\mathrm{lockin}})\) increases strictly toward the calibration value \(1\) as \(n\) approaches \(m_{\mathrm{lockin}}\) from below.
\end{proposition}

\paragraph{Honesty boundary (``first principles'' vs.\ calibration).}

\section{Conclusion}

The first-principles content formalised today is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a rigid combinatorial imprint norm \(\mathcal{N}_{\mathrm{curv}}=6^7\sqrt{3}\) with an octonionic/quaternionic contrast theorem;
\item a discrete cumulative curvature integral \(I(n)\) and horizon ratios \(\Omega_k(n;N)\) with proved strict monotonicity of \(I\), monotonicity (and strict monotonicity) of \(\Omega_k\) in the readout shell, the bound \(\Omega_k(n;N)\le 1\) for \(n\le N\), and lock-in calibration \(\Omega_k=1\) at \(m_{\mathrm{lockin}}\);
\item a parallel \(\eta(n;N)\) law sharing the \emph{identical} normalization as \(\Omega_k(n;N)\), with matching monotonicity lemmas and \(\eta(n;N)\le \eta_{\mathrm{paper}}\) for \(n\le N\), and with \(\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}\) quarantined to a single module;
\item a triality representation scaffold coupling curvature-ratio bias to CP-sensitive increments, without importing \(\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}\).
  This scaffold ties CP-odd increments to the same curvature-ratio channel used for \(\mathrm{Spin}(8)\) closure and measurement; it does \emph{not} derive the bias itself from non-associativity of the octonion product (that remains a Tier-IV target).
\item a GR/O--Maxwell compatibility package tying \((\alpha,\phi)\) across sectors.
\end{enumerate}
That package is already enough for a mathematically disciplined ``baryogenesis stands on the same shoulders as \(\mathrm{Spin}(8)\) closure'' paper; the remaining distance to textbook electroweak baryogenesis is the gap between ratio-level bookkeeping (already formalised) and yield-level dynamics (washout, sphaleron integration, and associator-derived CP violation), all of which remain outside the current Lean corpus.
